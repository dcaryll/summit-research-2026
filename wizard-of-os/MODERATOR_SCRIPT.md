# Moderator script — AI prompt study (wizard-of-os)

Everyone follows the **same flow** (no topic choice). Read **[brackets]** as your own words. *Italicized lines* are optional.

---

## After consent — everyone hears this first

“Thanks for being here. This station is **one continuous flow**—about **[8–12] minutes**.

First you’ll type **a prompt you’d honestly use**—the kind of thing you’d type to an AI or into Red Hat to get something done. Then you’ll answer **seven short question screens**—mostly taps—about **how** you like answers delivered and **how** you work. At the end there’s a **short summary screen**—it’s motivational, not a test.

There are no wrong answers. I’ll stay nearby—wave if you get stuck.”

---

## Prompt screen

“Go ahead and type **your** prompt in the box. **Real** beats perfect—use words you’d actually use today.”

*If they freeze:* “For example, you might ask how to **[solve a problem]**, compare **[two options]**, or **plan a migration**—whatever fits your job.”

*Think-aloud (if your protocol uses it):* “If you can, **say out loud** what you’re trying to accomplish before you submit—then put the prompt in the box.”

*Empty box:* “We need **at least a sentence or two** so we have something real to react to.”

*After they submit:* “Short loading moment—then the questions start.”

---

## Questions 1–7 (same order for everyone)

**You don’t need to read every option unless they ask.** Default: stay quiet.

**If they’re confused:** “In plain English: this one is about **[format / situation / how much control / your role / …]**—pick the option that’s **closest** to you.”

**If Next won’t work:** “If you picked **Other**, add a **short line** so the page knows what you mean.”

**If they need to fix the prompt mid-way:** “Use **Adjust input** if you see it—that takes you back to the prompt without losing everything.”

**Rough order of topics** (so you can answer “what’s next?”): *how they want content delivered* → *what situation they’re in* → *how much automation they want* → *their kind of role* → *how they influence buying* → *how long they’ll spend looking* → *where they’d go next for help.*

---

## Analyzing → dashboard

*Short wait.* “That screen is just **saving**—a second.”

**On the dashboard:** “This screen shows **patterns**—**common words** from prompts people have typed on this kiosk, and some **simple counts** from recent sessions. **Your exact prompt** isn’t shown here; it’s in our research data only.”

*If the word cloud looks empty:* “Sometimes it fills in as more people participate—that’s normal.”

---

## Closing

“That’s everything for this station—**thank you**. **[Swag / where to go next]** per our event.”

“When you’re done, tap **Start over** so the next person gets a clean screen—or I can help.”

---

## If something goes wrong (participant-facing language)

| Situation | What to say |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Next won’t work** | “Something’s still missing—often **Other** needs a line of text, or a choice isn’t selected.” |
| **They don’t understand a label** | Paraphrase in plain language; don’t change the answer choices. |
| **They’re anxious about the dashboard** | “Nothing here is grading you—it’s just showing **themes** from the kiosk.” |
