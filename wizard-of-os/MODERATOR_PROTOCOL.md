# Onsite moderator guide — AI prompt study (wizard-of-os)

Use this **during live sessions** with participants. Pair it with your consent script and incentive plan. It does **not** replace technical setup docs for your team.

---

## What you’re facilitating

Everyone follows the **same path**: type a **realistic prompt** (what they’d ask an AI or Red Hat), answer **seven short question screens**, then see a **summary dashboard** that shows patterns across prompts. Your job is to **welcome**, **set expectations**, **coach the opening prompt** if needed, **unblock** the flow, and **close**—without typing their prompt for them (unless they need an accessibility accommodation).

---

## One session, end to end

1. **Greet & consent** — Handle consent, NDA, and incentives before they start.
2. **Frame the task** — They’ll type **their own** prompt, then answer questions about **how** they like to get answers (format, situation, autonomy, role, etc.). Rough time (e.g. 8–12 minutes). No trick questions.
3. **Prompt screen** — They must enter **non-empty** text, then submit. Rotating **examples** on screen are hints only.
4. **Seven question screens** — One topic per screen; they use **Next** / **Previous** as needed. Last step leads to a short **“Analyzing”** moment, then the dashboard.
5. **Dashboard** — Reassuring headline and two kinds of visuals: **common words** from prompts on this device over time, and **simple stats** from recent sessions. **Not** their raw prompt on display.
6. **Done** — Thank them, next steps (swag, etc.). **Start over** clears the station for the next participant.

---

## When to step in

| Situation | You |
|-----------|-----|
| **Before they type** | **Frame** the prompt: real task they’d try today; think-aloud if your protocol uses it. |
| **Prompt screen** | **Main coaching moment** if they freeze—offer **categories** of examples, not a fake answer for them. Empty submit isn’t allowed; they need at least a sentence or two. |
| **Questions** | **Stay quiet** unless they’re confused or **Next** won’t work. **Other** often needs a line of text. If a label is jargon-heavy, paraphrase in plain language—don’t change the answer options. |
| **“Adjust input”** | If they need to **fix the prompt** after starting, point them to this control instead of refreshing the browser. |
| **Dashboard** | **Set expectations:** the word cloud draws from **earlier people on this kiosk**, not only them; it’s motivational. Their exact words stay in research data, not on this summary. |
| **Closing** | Thank them; **Start over** for the next person when the station is free. |

---

## What the seven questions are about (in order)

So you can anticipate—not read aloud unless asked:

1. **Format** — How they want an answer delivered (summary, deep dive, code, visuals, interactive, or other).
2. **Situation** — What they’re doing when they need help (e.g. budget, outage, research—**Other** may need text).
3. **Autonomy** — How much control vs automation they want (scale).
4. **Persona** — Builder, operator, defender, strategist–style roles (cards on screen).
5. **Buying / influence role** — How they influence tech decisions.
6. **Time to find an answer** — From instant to giving up.
7. **Where they’d go next** — Search, chatbots, forums, peers, vendor support, etc.

---

## Scripted lines (adapt freely)

**Opening:** “About **[8–12] minutes**. First you’ll type **a prompt you’d honestly use**—like something you’d type to an AI or Red Hat—then **seven short taps**. There are no wrong answers.”

**Prompt:** “**Your** words—doesn’t have to be polished. What would you actually try to get done?”

**Stuck on Next:** “If you picked **Other**, add a **short line** so the page knows what you mean.”

**Dashboard:** “This screen shows **patterns**—words people have used on this kiosk and some **counts** from today. Your **exact prompt** isn’t displayed here; it’s in our research data only.”

**Closing:** “Thank you. **[Swag / next step.]** Tap **Start over** when you’re done so the next person gets a clean screen.”

---

## Privacy & respect

- Their **prompt is sensitive**—treat it that way in conversation and in how you describe what’s on screen.
- On a **shared** kiosk, the dashboard can reflect **others’** prompts in the word cloud—say so plainly if they ask.

---

## Staff note (not for participants)

Data handling and export are per your **research ops** process. Moderators focus on the **participant experience** above.
