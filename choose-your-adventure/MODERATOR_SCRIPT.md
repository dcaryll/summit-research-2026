# Moderator script — focus studies (choose-your-adventure)

Read **[brackets]** aloud as your own words. *Italicized lines* are optional or situational. This script matches the **paths and studies** in the app; trim sections your runbook doesn’t use.

---

## After consent — everyone hears this first

“Thanks for being here. In a moment you’ll use this screen **on your own**—I’m here if you get stuck.

You’ll **pick one research topic** that fits you today. Then you’ll see some questions, and **some topics** include a **short interactive preview**—you can click around like it’s a real webpage. After that, you’ll hit **Submit** and you’re done. Budget about **[10–20] minutes**; there are no wrong answers.

**Four ways to start**—pick whichever feels easiest:

1. **Tap a topic card** that sounds like you, then **Take the study**.
2. **Random study** if you’d rather we surprise you with a topic.
3. **Help me choose** if you want **three quick questions** first, then we’ll land you on a topic.
4. **Choose by role** if you’d rather pick **how you work** and we’ll match you to a study.

When you’re ready, go ahead.”

---

## Pathway A — They pick a topic card (default)

**If they hesitate:** “Skim the **titles**—each one is a different angle, like **My Red Hat**, **Product evaluation**, or **Content discovery**. Tap the one that feels closest to what you care about today.”

**After they tap a card:** “When you’re ready, tap **Take the study** on that card.”

*Short “Preparing” pause—then the study opens.*

**You can say:** “You’re in—**Current study** at the top shows which topic you picked. Use **Next** to move forward; **Previous** if you want to change something.”

---

## Pathway B — Random study

**If they choose Random:** “The app will **pick a topic for you** and open it after a short animation—no need to tap **Take the study**.”

*Wait for animation.*

**When the study opens:** “You landed on **[topic name]**—you’ll see it at the top of the screen. If that really doesn’t fit you, say so and we can **go back** to the start before you get too far—use **Back to study selection** at the top.”

---

## Pathway C — Help me choose (three questions)

**Opening:** “Tap **Help me choose**. You’ll get **three questions**—**Next** after each one. On the last one, tap **See my recommended study**.”

**Question 1 — “What best describes your goal today?”**  
*You don’t need to read every option unless they ask.*  
“Pick the one that’s **closest** to why you’re here—profile and personalization, **evaluating products**, **developer tools**, **portal**, **trials**, **marketing**, or **finding content**.”

**Question 2 — “How would you describe your experience with Red Hat products?”**  
“**New**, **some**, **regular**, or **expert**—whatever fits.”

**Question 3 — “Which study would you like to take?”**  
“**Same list** as the big cards—pick the one you actually want to do.”

**After Q3 — back on the main screen:**  
“The app should have **selected** that study for you. Tap **Take the study** on that card to begin.”

*If they don’t see which card:* “Look for the **highlighted** or **[study name]** card and tap **Take the study** there.”

---

## Pathway D — Choose by role

**Opening:** “Tap **Choose by role**.” Then a list of roles opens.

**Say:** “Tap the **one** that best describes you today.”

| If they tap… | The study that opens |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **Developer** | Developer program |
| **IT Ops / SRE** | My Red Hat |
| **Security professional** | User preferences |
| **Buyer / Procurement** | Product marketing |
| **Learning & content** | Content discovery |
| **Technical evaluator** | Product evaluation |
| **Trials & subscriptions** | My trials |

**After they tap:** “The study will **open on its own** in a second—no **Take the study** needed.”

*If the topic feels wrong:* “Before you answer much, you can use **Back to study selection** at the top to go back and pick another path.”

---

## What to say once the study is open (all pathways)

**General:** “Work through at your own pace. **Next** is at the bottom when you’re ready to move on. If **Next** won’t work, something on the page still needs an answer—often a **drag-and-drop** or a line for **Other**.”

**Interactive previews:** “When you see a **clickable preview** or frame in the middle, **use it like a real product**—tap around, then hit **Next** when you’re ready for the follow-up questions.”

*Optional think-aloud:* “If you’re comfortable, **say what you’re thinking** as you click—especially if something surprises you.”

---

## What each study is about—so you can set expectations in one line

Use these **only if** they ask “what will I be doing?” or look lost.

| Study (on screen) | One line you can say |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| **User preferences** | “We’re asking **where** you’d expect to manage account info, **what** should follow you across Red Hat sites, and **how you feel** about sharing preferences.” |
| **Product evaluation** | “We’re asking how you **evaluate** software—trust, sandboxes vs installs, and a **budget-style** exercise about what you’d want in a trial.” |
| **Developer program** | “We’re asking **which developer tools** you use and **how** those resources help you—or could help more.” |
| **My Red Hat** | “There are **three parts**—portable tools, **AI-style** dashboard ideas, and **proof of value** for renewals. Each part has **intro text**, a **clickable preview**, then **follow-up questions**.” |
| **My trials** | “We’re asking **how you start trials** and what’s **hard** or **could be better**.” |
| **Product marketing** | “We’re asking how **product names and menus** should work when you’re buying or researching, then **open-ended** feedback.” |
| **Content discovery** | “We’re asking **how you prefer to learn**—topic, **numbered** first pick + **why**, **optional** other numbers, **branching** factor ranking + optional **Other** line, **six-type** ranking for the topic, then **Yes/No** on wanting **more** content types and **specifics** if Yes.” |

---

## Closing — everyone

**When they reach the thank-you screen:** “You’re all set—**thank you**. **[Swag / where to go next]** per our event. There’s a **QR code** on screen if you want to **[join the research community]**—totally optional.”

**Reset:** “When you’re done reading, tap **Start over** so the next person gets a clean screen—or I can help with that.”

---

## If something goes wrong (participant-facing language)

| Situation | What to say |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Wrong topic** | “Use **Back to study selection** at the top **before** you submit—then we can pick a different study.” |
| **Next won’t work** | “Something’s still missing—usually a **row** you need to drag, or **Other** needs a **short line** of text.” |
| **Blank preview** | “Give it a second; try **scrolling** inside the box. If it’s still empty, I’ll get help from our team.” |
| **They’re overwhelmed** | “Take your time. There’s no timer; I’m right here.” |
